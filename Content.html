(when i had a little accident that ultimately costed me my minimum wage job. which i didn't lose solely by my own responsibilities. I blame that on the hospitals and ShareCare for their extremely careless people and systems for not communicating with each other to fill out paperwork.)

Currently, it's far from complete, but as a solo developer I feel the progress made within just 2 months and the quality of gameplay it contains speaks volumes for how much more i can do in a year if i wasn't in the situation i am in right now.

And to me, this project was only a learning experience, a prototype, but one that plan to turn into a full blown product.

Games today have gone stale. The new games coming out are just rehashes of previous games. I want to bring something that at least, feels new and is genuinely FUN. No unreasonable difficulty or some lame power gap to prevents players from progressing because of some exponently scaled stat. Progression should be made because of an individuals skills. Or for the less skilled, commitment to a grind to build a weapon perfect for certain scenarios through the use of this games developing crafting system.

And speaking of a crafting system. You know the generic "crafting system" that pretty much grinds down to just getting a bigger number for your health or damage? Just, no. that's boring. That's lazy programming. In fact, i can't even call that programming if the progression is so dang simple. It's stupid and i think it holds humanity back. With any increase to something, there should be trade-offs. At least something to equalize those benefits if such power is to be obtained, but ofcourse there will be progression that will make the game easier just like in life the more one practices their particular trade.

So, what is this game you might be wondering? It's a horde shooter done right. The enemies will be coded in a way that produces emergent behavior to prevent predictabilities like, lining up for perfect colaterals, no merging with one another because they dont collide with each other, latency or server issues won't be a hinderance to the fun making you miss shots, and certainly no path finding that jerks their positions back and forth to weave through shots because they seemingly take advantage of the digital worlds unnatural way of movement.

This is a game that will be built with the idea of scalability and modularity so that as a solo developer, I can easily add content and updates as fast as possible. Like, on demand.

Currently what mechanics are featured is an object placement system akin to uglyburger0's 3008 game (not stolen mind you, i had to nearly build my own system from the ground up), and a unique gun crafting system that allows for one to dissassemble and reassemble in any order and placement the player chooses. Soon i'll complete the next part of that crafting system to allow players to modify the shape of each of those individual parts that will affect the performance of their assembled weapon.

but before that, I need to convert the enemy npcs to client-side so that i can better control how much lag players would receive. Oh yeah, and lag or server performance will actually be a part of the gameplay. Because the theme of this game will conveniently revolve around computer qualities.

For now, i hope the game can be enjoyed as is while i work on other unrelated issues preventing me from prioritize development of this game.

Another solo project of mine is a Python based AGI alternative to the mainstream programs. i won't say exactly how and why it works but i can say what is wrong with what's in the public.

Well, maybe not exactly what is wrong but i do have a pretty good idea why they aren't good enough to be called, amazing. When teams work on collaborative projects, there comes moments when ideas clash. And even when everyone agrees upon standards or paradigms there are still slightly different interpretations per individual. This inevitably turns parts of the project into a blackbox for everyone not involved and sections not coded by contributors becomes foreign territory only understood to a "good enough" degree. But that isn't the biggest issue. It's the part where the code becomes so abstracted it blends all definitions and concepts into a metaphorical grey blob. Such an AI will never truly understand the complexities of how humans define the world around us and it's objects.

On top of that, it becomes even more complicated when concepts without a tangible physical form doesn't have a standing for explanation because ideas are simply theories even if we can prove them from every angle possible.

Don't get me wrong, the people who created machine learning and computer neural networks are amazingly smart and maybe even way out of my depth, but there is still that trend of people with big brains being unable to dumb things down to the simple man. Without a way to translate the complexities of existance itself, Their AI will never reach that Movie esk level of true-ness. Accuracy will always be around that 99.99% and that just isn't enough. 100% sounds immpossible but i'm would like to boldly claim, it is possible. And all it takes a little humanity for our new up coming robotic friends.
